The Seven Basics

Inspired by Paracelsus' seven basics of spagyric preparation, each painting was begun on its planet's astrological election and only worked on its planetary day. Before each session, I would invoke the planet using ceremonial magic and sing the Orphic Hymn while burning the appropriate incense.

☽ I · Moon

The Moon

48″ × 30″ · Oils, casein, silver leaf

Commenced July 2021 when the moon resided in Taurus, forming a harmonious sextile with the sun in Cancer. To honor the moon's cyclic nature, I worked in mischtechnik — applying casein during the waxing and full phases for luminosity, dark hues during the waning and new phases for shadow and depth. Finished with silver leaf, the metal closely tied to lunar symbolism.

☿ II · Mercury

Mercury

48″ × 30″ · Oils on canvas

Begun when Mercury was conjunct the ascendant in Gemini, and worked on exclusively on Wednesdays — the day traditionally associated with Mercury. The painting carries the essence of the messenger: the bridge between celestial and terrestrial realms, the quicksilver mind in motion.

♀ III · Venus

Venus

48″ × 30″ · Oils on canvas, copper leaf

Begun during Venus's rulership in Taurus, painted in malachite (green, composed of copper sulfide) and sealed with copper leaf during the Venus/Moon conjunction in Taurus. Copper is Venus's metal; green is her color. This piece honors the delicate equilibrium of love, harmony, and beauty — the Venusian gravity that draws the natural world together.

☉ IV · Sun

Sun

48″ × 30″ · Oils on canvas, gold leaf

Commenced August 8th — the Lions Gate Portal — and worked on Sundays from there. Yellow envelops the canvas, radiating the Sun's vitality and joy; gold leaf embellishes the composition, honoring the precious metal that has always carried the Sun's symbolism. Vitality, will, and the source of all becoming.

♂ V · Mars

Mars

48″ × 30″ · Oils on canvas

Begun during Mars's residency in Scorpio in November 2021, and painted on Tuesdays. The palette is bold red — cadmium reds and Mars black — built up with iron oxide pigment sourced from rocks in the Rocky Mountains. Iron is the metal symbolic of Mars's raw power; this piece grounds that power in the very Earth that embodies it.

♃ VI · Jupiter

Jupiter

48″ × 30″ · Oils on canvas

A celestial tapestry of blues with touches of yellow — begun when Jupiter first entered Pisces in 2021, painted on Thursdays. Lapis lazuli, the stone long associated with Jupiter, is infused throughout. Expansion, abundance, wisdom; the largeness of soul.

♄ VII · Saturn

Saturn

48″ × 30″ · Oils on canvas

Painted primarily during Saturn's residency in Aquarius and worked on exclusively on Saturdays. To embody Saturn's gravity, I used only bone black — pigment created from charred animal bones — paired with lead white for highlight. Time, structure, the ring of necessity; the cycle of life and death.
